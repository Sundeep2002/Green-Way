    • How To Grow Ridge Gourd Crop

    Introduction

    Ridge gourd is an important Indian vegetable crop that can be grown all year. Ridge gourd is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. The Ridge gourd vegetable is used as a vegetable in its green stage, and the leaves with stem are used as a vegetable. It is a well-known vegetable in southern and eastern India.

    Soil and climate conditions

    Climatic conditions –

    Ridge gourd thrives in warm, humid climates; the ideal temperature range for Ridge gourd cultivation is 25° to 35° C.

    Soil conditions –

    The soil or main field should be thoroughly prepared with 4 to 5 ploughings, and a pH range of 6.5 to 7.5 is ideal for Ridge gourd cultivation. This vegetable crop necessitates adequate drainage. Applying organic matter or farmyard manure can increase vegetable yield and quality (FMY).

    Cultivation –

    Shallow cultivation is preferred during the early stages of plant growth. For better creeping, the plants should be given appropriate support in the form of bamboo sticks.

    Sowing -

    Ridge gourd seeds are planted by dibbling at 1.5 m to 2.0 m X 1.0 m to 1.5 m. In each pit, plant three seeds. Thin to 2 healthy seedlings after germination. Poly bags can also be used to sow the seeds. 2 seeds per bag are ideal, and 2 weeks after germination, they can be planted in the main field at 2 seedlings per pit.

    Fertilizer

    Manures and Fertilizers in Ridge Gourd Cultivation: Farm Yard 40 to 50 cartloads well decomposed Manure should be applied during the soil or field preparation process. Using the split application method, apply a fertiliser dose of 250:100:100 kg NPK / ha throughout the Ridge gourd cropping period.
