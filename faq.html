    Agriculture is the art and science of cultivating the soil, growing crops and raising livestock. It includes the preparation of plant and animal products for people to use and their distribution to markets. Agriculture provides most of the world's food and fabrics.

    “Farming is the act or process of working the ground, planting seeds, and growing food plants,” according to the dictionary definition. Farming can also refer to the practise of raising animals for milk or meat... “Farming is an excellent term for describing the lifestyle and work of people who work in the agriculture industry.” The noun "farm," which comes from the Medieval Latin Firma, was first used in the Middle Ages.

    Agriculture is the largest provider of livelihood in rural India. It contributes 25 percent to India’s GDP. It is still dependent primarily on the monsoons. The growth in agricultural production has been stagnant for the past several years. The drought in north and western parts in FY09 created shortages in supply of food grains.

    Agriculture is an important part of the economy of developing countries, as it is the primary source of food, income, and work for rural people. Agriculture is a major source of income for many countries, and these countries also contribute to the food supply.

    Soil health, also known as soil quality, refers to the soil's ability to function as an ecosystem for plants, animals, and humans. The term "soil health" is used by farmers to remind them that the soil includes living organisms that aid in the production of food.

    Soil health is influenced in three ways by farming: Nutrient supplementation. To increase crop yields, farmers frequently add nutrients to the soil. However, some of these nutrients and fertilisers can interfere with the ability of soil microorganisms to generate natural substances that crops require.

    Pesticides are chemicals that are used to keep hazardous plants and animals from harming your crops. Pesticides are divided into categories: Insecticides. Insecticides are chemicals that kill or repel insects. Herbicides. Weeds and other hazardous plants are killed or repelled by these compounds. Rodenticides. Rodents are killed or repelled by these compounds. Bactericides. A substance which kills bacteria.

    Both the environment and people's health are endangered by herbicides and pesticides. Because of runoff, leeching, and volatilization, these compounds are hazardous to the environment. These hazardous chemicals are sprayed on crops before being washed into rivers, ponds, and oceans, poisoning the entire body of water. Pesticides also have the potential to harm the nervous system, the reproductive system, and the endocrine system
